My Daily Routine
Your personalised schedule — tailored to your research life.
Morning (7:30 → 9:30 AM)
7:30 – 7:45
Wake + Hydrate
Drink a full glass of water. Open curtains, get light. No phone. Cortisol peaks in the first 30 min — light and hydration anchor your natural alertness.
7:45 – 8:10
Hygiene + Light Movement
Shower, get dressed, 5 min of stretching or slow walking. Body temperature change after showering signals wakefulness better than caffeine alone.
8:10 – 8:40
Proper Breakfast (sit down, no screens)
Protein + complex carbs + fat. Stabilises blood sugar for 3–4 hours — exactly the window you need for your first deep work block. Rushing breakfast causes a focus crash around 10 AM.
8:40 – 9:00
Morning Grounding
Write one intention for the day. Play guitar for 10–15 min casually. This primes your working memory before the lab throws things at you.
9:00 – 9:20
Research Planning
Review tasks. Identify ONE most important output for the day. Block your 8 hours deliberately. Deciding before work begins eliminates decision fatigue mid-session.
9:20 – 9:30
Settle In — Threshold Ritual
Make tea or coffee. Get to your workspace. Open your tools. A brief consistent ritual signals your nervous system that deep work is beginning.
Research Block (9:30 AM → 6:30 PM)
9:30 – 12:30
Deep Zone — YOUR Research
Cognitive security, persuasive AI, independent work. Hardest thinking, writing, math. Your brain is sharpest here. Protect this block fiercely.
11:00 – 12:30
Tue/Thu: Study Group (Mathematical Modelling)
Shift your deep work to 9:30–10:45 on these days. Group runs 11–12:30, then lunch immediately after.
12:30 – 1:00
Lunch — sit down, step away
Real break. Not at your desk. Your brain needs to downshift to consolidate the morning's work.
1:00 – 2:30
Collaborative / Bee Project
Structured tasks, data pipelines, hands-on bee work. Needs less raw focus than deep research — well-placed in the post-lunch window.
2:30 – 5:30
Output Zone — Reading, Writing, Coding
Afternoon energy dip recovers around 2:30. Use it for processing (papers, math, coding) rather than generating new ideas.
5:30 – 6:30
Wind-Down — Review + Research Diary
Summarise the day's output, fill your research diary, plan tomorrow's ONE thing. Closing the loop prevents lingering anxiety at night.
Evening (6:30 PM → 11:30 PM)
6:30 – 7:30
Dinner — cook if you want
Cooking is decompression. Take your time. Eating at a consistent hour helps circadian rhythm and sleep quality.
7:30 – 8:30
Free Time — Walk, Movie, Music
This is not optional. Genuine rest prevents burnout. Walking outside at dusk is one of the most effective mood regulators known.
8:30 – 9:30
Guitar Practice + Creative Time
30–45 min of structured practice. Creative activities at night — when the day's pressure is off — tend to be more expressive and enjoyable.
9:30 – 10:30
Wind Down — Evening Reflection
Fill your daily notes. Let phone use naturally reduce. Your nervous system needs a gradient into sleep, not a hard cutoff.
10:30 – 11:30
No Phone Hour
Read fiction, journal lightly, stretch. Blue light and social media activate your stress response. This hour is what makes sleep actually restful.
11:30 PM
Sleep
Non-negotiable. Memory consolidation — including what you studied — happens here. Cognitive performance tanks after 6 hours. Protect this.
Sustainability rules
Rule 1
Miss a day? Reset the next morning.
No guilt spirals. The goal is the average over weeks, not perfection every day.
Rule 2
One non-negotiable per day.
If everything falls apart, still do: breakfast + 4 hours research. That's the floor.
Rule 3
Weekends are lighter.
4–5 hours research max. More guitar, more walking, more rest. You are a person, not a productivity machine.
